# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users
A student or early-career professional tracking multiple accounts (cash, bank, card) who wants visual clarity instead of spreadsheet fatigue. The primary surface is the app itself (dashboard, transactions, budgets). A public landing page exists because this is also a portfolio piece shown to hiring managers.

## Product Purpose
A personal finance tracker that turns money data into a bold, legible bento-grid dashboard. Core loop: add a transaction (manual or import) → categorize it → see spend vs budget in real time → adjust budgets and goals from the trend. Success for MVP: add/edit/delete transactions, category breakdown chart, per-category budgets with progress, working auth with per-user data, and a live deployable URL.

## Positioning
Most trackers are spreadsheet clones. This one makes your money feel alive — a maximalist bento dashboard that stays ruthlessly legible where the numbers matter.

## Brand Personality
Bold, expressive, confident — but never at the cost of readability. Maximalist energy held together by strict hierarchy. Three words: **bold, dense, legible**.

## Anti-references
- Boring spreadsheet clones / default SaaS dashboards (flat gray, tiny type, no identity).
- Pastel, low-contrast "calm finance" looks that hide the numbers.
- Thin-line minimalism that reads as unbuilt / AI slop.
- Gradient-text eyebrows and identical card grids.

## Design Principles
- Hierarchy controls the chaos: maximalism is allowed only because size, weight, and color rank every element.
- Numbers are the hero. Display type, tabular alignment, and count-up motion make balances unmissable.
- Consistency is the discipline: one grid gap, one radius, one shadow language keep density from becoming clutter.
- Legibility is non-negotiable on money. No aesthetic choice may reduce contrast on a real balance.
- Motion earns its place: staggered, intentional, never overwhelming; respects reduced-motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, muted text tuned to clear the secondary bar. Full keyboard access for modals, dropdowns, and navigation. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color is never the only signal — categories pair color with icon and label.
